Summary Insights

Hospital

I visited Calicut Beach Hospital in Kozhikode, India, for a second time. I had an X-ray scan for IVF and IDF. The hospital is one of the best hospitals in the world and a great place to get treatment.

Metro

Bangalore Metro is safe but too crowded. The crowd is not fast, cost-efficient, and somewhat clean. It was good, efficient, and fast. I feel it is comfortable, safe, and cost-effective, though the timing could be improved.

Public Trains

Facilities are small in number but useful, like a bathroom and kitchen. The seats are not comfortable, and there is a rush, but it is reasonably priced. General class lacks hygiene and safety, with poorly maintained toilets.

Roads

India's national highway network has transformed, but NHAI has some of the worst highways in the world. While India is a cosmopolitan country, it lacks infrastructure. It’s disappointing that the government hasn’t prioritized repairs.

Schools

Nadakkav School provides a nurturing environment with skilled teachers, a well-maintained campus, excellent staff, and a high standard of education.

Toilets

Public toilets are often unhygienic and poorly maintained. They need significant improvement in cleanliness and maintenance, as they are smelly and moderately clean in most cases.

Water Supply

Water shortage is a reality in Indian cities, including Bangalore. The U.S. also faces water crises, with toxic Yamuna water in Delhi and concerns in Rajasthan’s capital and Hyderabad.

Sewage System

Sewage systems in cities are outdated, causing frequent overflows. Investments in modern infrastructure and public-private partnerships are essential to prevent river and lake pollution.
